Alcohol

WHAT IS ALCOHOL?

Alcohol is classified as a depressant drug, meaning that consuming alcohol slows down the body's functions. The user would feel droswy, their movement might feel unsteady, and would not react quickly. Alcohol is found in drinks such as beer, wine, spirits, cider, etc.

Although there are certainly some who like drinking alcohol, there are many risk factors included with drinking. The liver can only process one "standard" drink (14g of alcohol) in an hour, and consuming more than that could affect the person's health. (More on the consequences could be found below)















INFLUENCES

There are numerous influences that could cause people to drink. Below is a list of major influences that are the most common for teens. However, remember that not all influences are listed here. If you see that you are heavily influenced by one of the categories, you should remember to be aware of that.


Peer Pressure

Peer pressure is one of the biggest influences for drug use among teenagers. Because popularity is very significant to most teenagers, they might try taking drugs in order to seem cool and fit. Furthermore, friends might also try to persuade you into drinking alcohol.

Friends might also try to persuade you into drinking alcohol. They might say that you will be cool if you drink, or that drinking is fun.



Media

Media such as movies and TV shows sometimes make it seem like taking drugs is an okay thing to do. The user might take a high dosage of drugs and get away with absolutely no consequences. Because of this, people might think that they will also take drugs without having consequences too.


Stress

Stress is extremely common in teenagers. They have to get good grades, undergo peer pressure, and have to do well in extra-curricular activities such as sports.

Since alcohol is a depressant, it slows down the bodily functions and the ability to process information. Therefore, stressed teenagers feel less stressed when drinking and feel as if their problems don't exist. However, their problems still do exist - and drinkers will most definitely have more problems if they are caught drinking.


Excitement

For those who enjoy the 'thrill' of life and crave excitement, they might want to try alcohol. Alcohol gives teens something to do and could bring excitement in some ways. Others who easily feel bored and trouble being occupied might also be tempted to try drinking.


Curiosity

Some people are just naturally curious about what alcohol does, and that may cause them to try alcohol themselves. They might just want to drink a bit, but they may even get addicted to alcohol just by drinking a small amount. So after drinking the first time, it is not a matter of curiosity anymore - it just feels good.








Accessibility

Alcohol is much more accessible than other illegal drugs, since it is legal in most countries and there are many drinks that contain it. Although it is illegal for teens to drink in almost all countries, it is still much easier to possess alcohol than other illegal drugs because of how accessible it is.















CONSEQUENCES

There is a plethora of consequences that come with drinking. The consequences are separated below into two catergories: short term and long term. If you do consume alcohol, remember to take it in small doses to avoid harmful consequences.


Short Term

As touched on above, alcohol is a depressant and slows down the bodily functions. If a low dosage is consumed, the user will feel relaxed and calm (and possibly be happy because of that), and they will have a slower reaction time than usual. The user's coordination will also be slightly reduced.

If a medium dosage is consumed, the user might feel sleepy, and they will have slurred speech and blurry vision. Users could also have altered emotions and act moodily; they might be tempted to behave irrationally.

If a high dosage is consumed, the user will vomit uncontrollably. He/she would be disoriented, have breathing difficulties, and might even lose consciousness. Extremely high dosages will result in comas, blackouts, and death.


Long Term

Most of these consequences occur only if the user continues to abuse alcohol. Brain damage would be done, and normal brain development will be disrupted - affecting the user academically. More serious health issues could develop, such as throat/mouth cancer, liver disease, a vitamin B1 deficiency, ulcers, gastritis, and various heart-related diseases.

Some alcohol abusers might feel happy from drinking so much. However, most people who abuse alcohol will feel stressed and sad due to the the inability to quit drinking. Relationships and friendships could be harmed and broken, as not many people will approve of the user's abuse.

Drinking during pregnancy could lead to a child with 'Fetal Alcohol Syndrome' (FAS). Children with FAS could grow less quickly, have problems with their nervous systems, have facial abnormalities, and have mental retardation.













WHAT CAN BE DONE TO STOP IT



GROUNDCOVER

Groundcover is a blanket of low-growing plants that protect the topsoil from numerous things. Groundcover is good for a number of reasons. First of all, groundcover prevents erosion from happening, especially wind and water erosion. The plants also improves water inflitration by intercepting raindrops and slows down water evaporation, which in turn helps soil remain moist for a longer period of time. in addition to that, groundcover buffers temperatures for the topsoil, which ensures safe temperatures for topsoil to rebuild if it is lost.








THE GOVERNMENT

The government has the most influence over a country, and therefore can almost always do the most to help a specific topic - which is exactly what the government should do. The government can regulate laws regarding good irrigation techniques and restricting overgrazing, overcutting, and overuse of agrochemicals on vegetation. Even though in the short term these actions will have negative effects, but in the long term, these actions will have major positive effects on the restoration of the soil.









RAISING AWARENESSS

It is not just the government that should be aware of and try to resolve this issue - everyone should be. There are many ways to make everyone informed about land degradation, and here are some ways how. Students in schools should be taught about this with the help of direct education. NGO's should raise awareness by addressing the importance and consequences of this issue to both the public and individual groups, such as schools and community centers, and create campaigns about this topic as well. Lastly, well-known figures should spread knowledge and raise awareness about this.

Third World

Third world countries are the ones that take a toll. Unlike richer countries, countries of the third world are less able to fix the issue of degradation do to the lack of money.
